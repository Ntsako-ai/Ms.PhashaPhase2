About Phapano Pasha

Phapano Pasha is a distinguished strategic communications specialist, political advisor, and thought leader with over 25 years of experience across politics, academia, business, and media. Her expertise lies in communication strategy, political campaigns, scenario planning, crisis and reputation management, speechwriting, stakeholder engagement, policy development, research, and media campaigns.

With roots in grassroots activism spanning over two decades, Phapano transitioned her leadership into business and strategic advisory, while continuing to shape debates on democracy, development, and African renewal.

Career Highlights & Leadership

Founder & CEO – PM Pasha Consulting

Providing high-level strategic communications, crisis management, confidential speechwriting, and advisory services for political leaders, institutions, and private clients.

Chairperson – Centre for Alternative Political and Economic Thought (CAPET)

Steering pioneering research on Global South and BRICS nations, while advising policymakers on innovative approaches to governance, economics, and international relations.

ANC Johannesburg Region – Former Head of Scenario Planning, Communication & Research

Developed strategic frameworks and research-driven solutions for the party’s policy and communications arm.

Media & Thought Leadership

Phapano has authored and published over 120 articles, podcasts, interviews, and research pieces through her platform Global Africa: African Voices Matter. Her commentary and publications focus on elevating African perspectives in global debates, challenging stereotypes, and driving fresh approaches to social, political, and economic reform.

Her thought leadership spans industries including politics, public sector, higher education, international relations, energy, civil society, arts, and media. Her work resonates nationally, continentally, and globally, positioning her as one of Africa’s most consistent and visionary voices.

Vision & Advocacy

Phapano’s vision is rooted in building a self-sufficient Africa that invests in its people, amplifies African voices, and reshapes narratives about the continent on the global stage. She continues to use her platforms to nurture new leaders, influence policy, and ensure Africa’s voice matters in the shaping of tomorrow’s world.

At the core of her work is a belief in an Africa that leads with vision, resilience, and unity — a continent whose voices matter.
